### 2. Direct answer block (AEO)
- Answers "How much does programmatic advertising cost?" This is the citable block; it must be true generically even before GM's own model is stated.
- RESOLVED 2026-07-06. GM does not publish a fee model, but the live pricing page publishes effective-rate benchmarks by channel with an all-inclusive framing and no long-term contracts (now in brand/facts.md). The answer block uses those.
- Draft copy (56 words): "Programmatic advertising is typically priced on CPM, the cost per 1,000 impressions, or CPA, where you pay when someone takes an action. On the Genius Monkey platform, effective CPMs run $2 to $4 for display, $10 to $22 for video, $25 to $40 for OTT, and $21 to $37 for audio, all-inclusive, with no long-term contracts."

### 3. What drives your cost
- Intent: educational, citable, keeps the page honest. Short subsections or a table: budget and flight length, channel mix (CTV costs more per impression than display, does more per exposure), audience competition, creative formats.
- Lead (draft copy): "Four things move the number, how much you spend, where it runs, who you're trying to reach, and what the ad is. Everything else is packaging."
- Definitions of CPM and CPA use the verbatim key-term definitions from voice-and-tone.md (they carry the voice and are pre-approved). CPM definition ends "never the whole story," which sets up the next section.

### 4. How Genius Monkey charges

No rate-card fee tiers exist, and the copy says that plainly instead of hiding it.
- Include the per-channel benchmark table as copy (display, video, OTT, audio ranges plus cost per completed view for OTT). Note in the draft to confirm the ranges are still current before each publish.

### 5. Proof, the efficiency angle
- Intent: pricing pages get read skeptically, so show cost numbers, not revenue numbers. Use: BrickHouse Nutrition CPA from $83 to under $28 in three months. Freed-Hardeman cost per conversion down 89% in nine months. onsemi $2,000 to under $1.
- Lead (draft copy): "Price matters less than what the price buys. BrickHouse Nutrition paid $83 per acquisition when they arrived. Three months later, under $28."

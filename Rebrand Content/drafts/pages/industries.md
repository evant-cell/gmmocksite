A law firm's lead is not a car dealer's lead. Campaigns here get built around how your buyers actually decide, and we can show you the numbers from brands that look like yours. Find your industry below, or start with the case studies and work backward. (links: /programmatic-case-studies)

## Which industries use programmatic advertising

Programmatic advertising works in any industry where buyers research before they act. Genius Monkey runs campaigns for automotive, education, finance, legal, healthcare, political, travel, nonprofit, home services, and retail brands, and tunes targeting, channels, and measurement to how each vertical's buyers actually decide.

## Why your industry changes the build

Ashley Furniture told us more than 40% of their customers take 90 days to convert. When a university client added OTT, 22.6% of conversions started landing inside 2 days. If your advertising treats those two buyers the same, one of them is wasting money.

Conversion windows are the obvious difference. Compliance rules, channel mixes, and what even counts as a lead differ just as much. That's also why the proof below is matched to the vertical. A cross-industry average flatters everyone and informs no one.

### Automotive
(links: /industries/automotive)
A southwestern dealership cut its cost per lead 54%, from $124.35 to $71.27. The same campaigns cut the cost of a website visitor from $5.81 to $2.39. Car buyers shop for weeks across every screen they own, and the campaigns are built for exactly that.

### Education
(links: /industries/education)
Freed-Hardeman University cut its cost per conversion 89% in nine months. Most online graduate students search within 150 miles of home, and Freed-Hardeman sits in rural West Tennessee. The campaigns found their students anyway.

### Finance
(links: /industries/finance)
No published finance study yet, and we won't pretend otherwise. The nearest neighbor is B2B, where onsemi, a Fortune 500 semiconductor company, went from $2,000 per conversion to $8, then under $1 two years later.

### Legal
(links: /industries/legal)
A personal injury firm cut its display cost per lead 50% and video 68%, inside six months. Eight weeks in, display leads cost $10 and video $19. A few months later, $5 and $6. Legal clicks are some of the most expensive on the internet, which makes wasted ones unforgivable.

### Healthcare and medical
(links: /industries/healthcare)
A telemedicine company grew patient visits 150% with a 300% return on ad spend. That client treats 70+ conditions online nationwide, so the audience was everywhere. Health audiences need reach and discretion at the same time, and the targeting delivers both.

### Political
(links: /industries/political)
Candidates on the platform have an 81.5% average win rate, and in one swing state, a PAC client drove 18% cross-ticket movement. For the same budget, campaigns reach 2X the audience of other digital buys and 6X traditional TV, radio, or newspaper.

### Travel
(links: /industries/travel)
No published travel study yet. The nearest proof is events, where Country Thunder turned every ad dollar into $230 of ticket sales. Trip planners research the way ticket buyers do, across weeks and across screens.

### Nonprofit
(links: /industries/nonprofit)
Trees for the Future grew donations 46% on their first campaign. ACSTO grew its donor base when fewer than 5% of Arizona taxpayers participated in the program it depends on. Nonprofit budgets are small and watched closely, so efficiency isn't a nice-to-have.

### Home services
(links: /industries/home-services)
Cenvar Roofing and Solar grew website leads 44% in 45 days, then gross billing 30% over a year. One report traced 57 interactions that started from a single Genius Monkey ad. Home services leads move fast, and the advertising has to keep up.

### Retail and ecommerce
(links: /industries/retail)
Canales Furniture, a family-owned retailer with 17 locations around Dallas Fort Worth, grew new website visitors 887% and in-store visitors 900%. Retail proof shows up at the register, not just in sessions, so that's where we measure.

# Programmatic advertising pricing, explained before the sales call

Most platforms make you book a meeting to learn what a meeting costs. Here's how the pricing actually works.

## How much does programmatic advertising cost

Programmatic advertising is typically priced on CPM, the cost per 1,000 impressions, or CPA, where you pay when someone takes an action. On the Genius Monkey platform, effective CPMs run $2 to $4 for display, $10 to $22 for video, $25 to $40 for OTT, and $21 to $37 for audio, all-inclusive, with no long-term contracts.

## What drives your cost

Four things move the number, how much you spend, where it runs, who you're trying to reach, and what the ad is. Everything else is packaging.

### Budget and flight length

Longer flights give optimization room to compound. Country Thunder's conversion cycle reporting found a sweet spot of 25 to 60 days of carefully placed ads before a ticket sale. Cutting a flight short doesn't save money. It stops the learning right before it pays.

### Channel mix

CPM is the cost per 1,000 ad impressions, a baseline for comparing media costs across channels, useful for planning, but never the whole story. CTV costs more per impression than display and does more per exposure. A completed 15-second spot on a television is not a banner glance. We look at CPM in context of real conversion data, not in isolation.

### Audience competition

The more advertisers want the same person you do, the more each impression costs. Auctions are auctions. Sharper targeting doesn't dodge the auction, it makes sure you're only bidding on people worth winning.

### Creative format

Video costs more to serve than display, and OTT more than both. On OTT you can pay per completed view, $0.04 to $0.08, so the premium buys a finished message, not a maybe.

## Pricing questions, answered straight

### How much does programmatic advertising cost?

Cost scales with your budget and channel mix rather than a fixed fee schedule. On Genius Monkey, effective CPMs run $2 to $4 for display, $10 to $22 for video, $25 to $40 for OTT, and $21 to $37 for audio, with display clicks averaging $0.60 to $1.09, all-inclusive.

### What is a typical programmatic CPM?

CPM is the cost per 1,000 impressions, and it varies by channel. Published Genius Monkey benchmarks run $2 to $4 for display, $10 to $22 for video, $25 to $40 for OTT, and $21 to $37 for audio. Those are effective rates, what clients actually see, not a teaser number.

### What's included in the price?

Everything. The rates above are all-inclusive, meaning the platform, the expert running your account, the optimization, and the reporting that ties spend to results are all in the number. There are no setup fees and no charges that appear once you're committed.

### CPM vs CPA, which will I pay?

CPM is the cost per 1,000 ad impressions, useful for planning but never the whole story. CPA charges only when a desired action, a purchase, form fill, or signup, is completed. You pay for results, not just exposure. We plan on CPM benchmarks and judge on conversion data, so both numbers stay honest.

### Do I have to sign a long-term contract?

No. Genius Monkey doesn't use long-term contracts. Campaigns earn the renewal or they don't, and the transparent reporting makes that call an easy one either way. If the numbers hold up, you'll want to stay. If they don't, nothing traps you here.

## Or pick your channel

Every channel page goes deep on formats, targeting, and what the channel is for. Here's the short version of each.

### Display
(links: /solutions/by-channel/display)
Banner and rich media ads across the sites and apps your buyers already use. The widest reach in programmatic, and usually the cheapest way to test a new audience.

### OTT and CTV
(links: /solutions/by-channel/ott-ctv)
Ads on streaming TV, from smart TVs to Roku and Apple TV. The reach of broadcast with the precision of digital targeting, and one of the fastest-growing channels we operate in.

### Video
(links: /solutions/by-channel/video)
Pre-roll and in-stream video across the open web. Motion and sound for the message a banner can't carry, measured to the conversion like everything else here.

### Native
(links: /solutions/by-channel/native)
Ads that match the look and feel of the page they sit on. Less interruption, more attention, and the same data behind the targeting.

### Audio
(links: /solutions/by-channel/audio)
Streaming audio and podcast ads that reach people while they drive, run, and cook. Screens off, attention on.

## Channels work as a system

Nobody watches one screen anymore, so we don't buy one screen. Your buyers move from phone to laptop to TV without a second thought, and cross-device targeting follows that journey without losing the thread. The budget follows the buyer, not the media plan. (links: /platform/device-agnostic-targeting)

The numbers back the opinion. When a university client added OTT to their display and video campaigns, conversions landing inside 2 days went from 5% to 22.6%. Same audience, better system. It's also why we'd rather run three channels well than one channel loudly. (links: university OTT case study)

## The guides

We helped coin the term programmatic advertising back in 2010. You name it, you explain it. Four guides, written so the definitions stop drifting.

### What is programmatic advertising
(links: /resources/guides/what-is-programmatic-advertising)
The automated buying and selling of digital advertising using real-time data and technology. Our definition, in the most literal sense. The guide runs it from one line to a working campaign.

### What is a DSP
(links: /resources/guides/what-is-a-dsp)
The platform advertisers use to buy ad impressions across exchanges through one interface. We built ours from scratch, so consider the guide a tour from the people who built the machine.

### What is CTV
(links: /resources/guides/what-is-ctv)
Ads on internet-connected TVs and streaming services. Broadcast reach, digital aim. We've run CTV since 2019, and the guide says where it belongs in a media plan and when it doesn't.

### Retail media, explained
(links: /resources/guides/retail-media)
Retailer ad networks, what they're good at, where they overlap with programmatic, and when they deserve budget. We have opinions. They're in there.

## Overview, the essence

Genius Monkey sounds like the smartest person in the room, and the most genuine one to talk to. We are direct, confident, and occasionally irreverent, but never corporate and never vague. Every word we publish should feel like it came from someone who deeply knows what they're doing, loves doing it, and shows up every day with their clients' best interests front of mind. We don't dress up uncertainty in jargon. We say what we mean, back it with data, and let our results do the loud talking.

**Personality:** Outspoken, Confident, Maverick, Approachable, Playful, Direct.

## Tone, the four pillars

- **Loud.** We speak boldly and without hesitation. Our messaging takes strong positions, celebrates leadership, and makes it clear we know what we're doing. We are better than most. This is not bluster. It is earned.
- **Humble.** Even when we lead loudly, we stay approachable. We never talk down or let ego eclipse the client. Strong statements are paired with openness, clarity, and respect for the people in the room.
- **Playful.** We mix confidence with personality. The tone can be fun, witty, and outspoken, while staying rooted in expertise and credibility. We're not a stuffed shirt. We're the person at the table everyone wants on their side.
- **Outspoken.** We say what others won't, and say it plainly. Our opinions show up in clear opinions and messaging that cuts through noise without hiding behind jargon or hedging with qualifications. We have a point of view. We share it.

## Tone calibration, positioning GM against enterprise-scale competitors

The target is "cool and confident." Three ways to miss it.

- **Too arrogant.** "Everyone else is doing it wrong, and we're tired of pretending that's debatable." Confidence without humility feels dismissive. Overstating dominance or belittling competitors erodes the trust we've spent years building.
- **Too safe.** "We aim to help improve your advertising efforts with thoughtful, data-informed solutions." Muted, overly cautious language buries leadership. Playing it safe means disappearing into the same noise as every other vendor.
- **Too corporate.** "Genius Monkey leverages integrated solutions to optimize omnichannel media initiatives at scale." Stiff, polished messaging strips away personality and disconnects the brand from the real, talented humans behind it.
- **Right, cool and confident.** "We don't compete in this space. We outgrow it while others try to catch up. No one else in programmatic even comes close to what we do, and we can back that up." If it's true and we can back it up, we are not afraid to say it plainly. Our confidence that comes with receipts isn't arrogance. It's leadership.

## Word choice

### Describing our clients
USE: Clients, Brands, Organizations, Growth-focused businesses, Partners.
AVOID: Customers, Accounts, Advertisers, Enterprises, Corporations, Conglomerates, Institutions.
Why: Too familiar feels transactional, too formal feels cold and distant. We are in a peer relationship, not a vendor one. "Clients" respects the relationship without overpromising friendship.

### Services (to clients)
USE: Programmatic platform, Advertising technology, Cross-device marketing, Data-driven advertising, Performance media.
AVOID: Solutions, Tools, Software, Systems, Individual user tracking, Surveillance-based targeting.
Why: Vague umbrella terms like "solutions" undersell our precision. Invasive-sounding language creates distrust. Name the work specifically, it signals expertise.

### Services (to consumers)
USE: Relevant advertising, Smarter ads, Better brand experiences, Ads that make sense.
AVOID: Tracking individuals, Watching user behavior, Following people online, Targeting algorithms, Data harvesting.
Why: Consumer-facing language must be honest, simple, and non-threatening. We serve people, not audiences.

### Describing our team and culture
USE: Our team, Our people, The experts behind the platform, Real humans who care.
AVOID: Resources, Staff, Headcount, Personnel, Human capital.
Why: The people at Genius Monkey are our greatest differentiator. Language about them should reflect that, warm, direct, human. "Resources" makes people sound like line items.

## Principles

- **Own your confidence.** If it's true and we can back it up, say it. Genius Monkey is a category leader. Write like one. Playing it safe is only a kind of dishonesty. If we've earned the right to say something bold, we say it.
- **Say it plainly.** Don't hide behind jargon or dress up uncertainty in acronyms. If something is complicated, explain it simply, that's what smart people do. Every reader is busy. Earn their attention by respecting their time.
- **Lead with outcomes.** Clients don't care about features. They care about results. Put the win first, then explain how we got there. "Your ROAS improved 40%" is more powerful than any product description.
- **Be human, always.** Behind every campaign is a person. Our tone is warm, direct, and free of corporate stiffness, because the humans on our team are too. Write like you'd talk to a smart colleague, not like you're writing a legal brief.
- **Punch above the noise.** We compete with companies that have ten times our resources. Our advantage is being sharper, faster, and more honest than anyone else in the room. Let that come through in every line, with the quiet certainty that comes from actually delivering.
- **Client interest is non-negotiable.** Every sentence that touches a client's brand should pass one test, is this in their best interest? If the answer is anything other than yes, rewrite it. This isn't a positioning strategy. It's how we're built.

## Copy gallery, approved examples

- **Advertising services.** "We help your ads show up in the right places, at the right time, on the screens people actually use. Instead of guessing what's working, you can actually see it. We turn complex advertising into clear outcomes, and our job isn't done until your spend is doing exactly what it should."
- **Innovation.** "Advertising changes fast, and so do we. Our technology is designed to adapt as devices, platforms, and behaviors shift. We don't chase trends, we set the pace. We solve advertising. Everyone else is still guessing."
- **Client promise.** "Every decision we make around your ad spend is based on one thing. Your best interest. That's not a tagline. It's how we're wired, and it's why clients stay."
- **Competitive position.** "We don't have the biggest war chest in this space. We have the sharpest team, the most transparent reporting, and a platform that actually performs. Our clients don't leave because they don't need to. That's the only metric that matters."
- **The people behind the platform.** "Every account has a real expert behind it, someone who treats your budget like it's their own. There is no faceless algorithm here. There are people who care, who pick up the phone, and who would never recommend something that wasn't right for your business."

## Key terms, how GM defines them

Use these definitions verbatim or close to it. They carry the voice.

- **Programmatic advertising.** The automated buying and selling of digital advertising using real-time data and technology. No phone calls, no manual insertion orders, just intelligent media buying at scale, targeted precisely and measured transparently.
- **DSP (demand-side platform).** The technology platform that allows advertisers to purchase ad impressions across multiple exchanges through one unified interface. Genius Monkey operates its own DSP, giving clients greater control, transparency, and performance than third-party platforms allow.
- **CPA (cost per acquisition).** A pricing model that charges only when a desired action, a purchase, form fill, or signup, is completed. You pay for results, not just exposure. A lower CPA means more customers for the same budget.
- **ROAS (return on ad spend).** Revenue earned for every dollar spent on advertising. If you spend $1 and earn $5, your ROAS is 5x. We track ROAS obsessively because it's the most honest measure of whether advertising is actually working.
- **Attribution.** The process of identifying which ad touchpoints contributed to a conversion. Good attribution tells the true story of what's actually working. It stops you from funding channels that look good on paper but don't move the needle.
- **Cross-device targeting.** The ability to reach the same person across phones, tablets, laptops, and connected TVs, following the real customer journey without losing the thread. Modern buyers don't sit still. Your advertising shouldn't either.
- **Viewability.** A measurement standard confirming that an ad was actually seen by a real person, not just technically served. We optimize for real eyes, not bot traffic or ads buried below the fold.
- **Brand safety.** The guardrails ensuring ads don't appear alongside harmful, inappropriate, or low-quality content. We take this seriously because your reputation matters to us. Protecting it is part of the job, not an upsell.
- **CPM (cost per mille).** The cost per 1,000 ad impressions. It's a baseline metric for comparing media costs across channels, useful for planning, but never the whole story. We look at CPM in context of real conversion data, not in isolation.
- **Connected TV (CTV).** Advertising delivered through internet-connected televisions, streaming services, smart TVs, and devices like Roku or Apple TV. CTV combines the reach of broadcast with the precision of digital targeting, and it's one of the fastest-growing channels we operate in.

## Who we talk to

- **Marketing agencies.** Agency partners who manage media on behalf of multiple brand clients. Performance-focused, resource-stretched, and need a technology partner that makes them look like heroes to their clients. They evaluate us on reliability, transparency, and how well we play on their team, not just how impressive our pitch deck is.
  - How we speak to them: Talk peer-to-peer. They are experts. Treat them that way. Emphasize how Genius Monkey makes agencies more competitive, not just more capable. Lead with efficiency gains, white-glove service, and reporting clarity their clients will actually notice. Never oversell. Agencies can smell a pitch from a mile away, and they will remember the promise you made against the results you delivered.
- **Organizations (direct-to-business).** Brand marketers, CMOs, and growth leaders at companies managing their own advertising. Balancing tight budgets against ambitious goals, may not have deep programmatic expertise in-house. They need someone they can trust to both execute and explain, without talking down to them.
  - How we speak to them: Simplify without dumbing down. Lead with outcomes and ROI, then back it with enough clarity that they understand the how. These clients need two things, confidence that their money is working hard, and the certainty that someone genuinely has their back, not just their invoice. Be the smart, honest partner who happens to be the best in the business.
- **Press and media.** Journalists, analysts, and industry commentators covering adtech, marketing technology, and business. They want access to data, genuine expert perspective, and stories that go beyond the press release. Their audiences are skeptical and informed, and so are they.
  - How we speak to them: Be a genuine resource, not a pitch machine. Share data, clear perspective, and real talk about where the programmatic industry is going. Avoid promotional language. Earned credibility with media comes from giving them something worth publishing, not from sending another sponsored opinion. Quote-worthy means direct, specific, and genuinely interesting. We have a strong point of view. Share it without spin.
